# Style DNA

## Visual Essence

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, high negative space, restrained, absurd, product-sketch feel, handwritten-style text, clear structure without looking like a textbook or user manual.

It should look like a sketch quickly drawn on a blank sheet of paper by a product designer, AI builder, developer, or creator to explain a point.

## Essential Criteria

- **16:9 Aspect Ratio:** Standalone horizontal illustrations (target resolution: 1125x633 pixels).
- **Pure White Background:** No beige, off-white, paper textures, gradients, drop shadows, noise, or vintage filters.
- **Black Hand-Drawn Line Art:** Main lines should be thin, slightly wobbly/imperfect, and hand-drawn. Avoid geometric vector lines, heavy strokes, or mechanical shapes.
- **High Negative Space:** The main subject should occupy around 40%–60% of the canvas. At least 35% of the canvas must remain completely blank/silent.
- **Sparse Latin-Script Annotations (Hinglish/Tenglish/Kanglish/English/etc.):** Maximum of 5–8 labels. Keep each label short (2–8 words) and written using the Latin script (English alphabet). Transliterating regional Indian words (like "Ruko", "Pani Shuru", "Duddu", "Kelsa") into English spelling is encouraged to ensure AI image generation models render the text correctly.
- **Single Core Metaphor:** One illustration explains exactly one core concept, process, state, or interaction. This metaphor can be a multi-step workflow with multiple connected mechanical elements (e.g., conveyor belts, gears, chutes, holes, boxes, ropes, pulleys) to show a progression or comparison. For technical architectures (like RAG, databases, cache, API routing), avoid drawing a single generic machine; break down the system into its core stages (e.g., ingestion, chunking, embedding, vector DB, retrieval) and represent each with its own wobbly physical counterpart.
- **Multiple Character Instances:** You may show one or more instances of the character Desi in different parts of the illustration to represent different roles, actions, or steps in the system (e.g., one Desi inputting material, another Desi catching output).
- **Concept Title:** Do not write generic category headers (like "Workflow", "System Architecture", "Pitfalls") in the top-left corner. However, a wobbly, handwritten main concept title describing the specific metaphor (e.g., "Two Breakpoints", "Trust Bridge", "Multilingual Sorting") is allowed and should be centered at the top of the illustration.

## Color Scheme

- **Black:** Main outline, character (Desi), boxes, lines, structures, and primary text/labels.
- **Orange:** Main flows, directions, automation paths, and movement arrows (from A to B).
- **Red:** Warnings, issues, blockages, emotional hotspots, critical rules, or negative results.
- **Blue:** Supplementary details, internal mental states, system feedback, secondary explanations, or AI/assistant/automation cues.

Blue is optional and does not need to be in every drawing. Use color sparingly—less is more.

## Strictly Prohibited

- No slick corporate vector styles or commercial marketing illustrations.
- No formal PowerPoint presentation slides or textbook charts.
- No formal UML, BPMN, or technical flowcharts.
- No cute, shiny, or childish mascot posters.
- No complex or technical architecture diagrams.
- No literal UI mockups or actual application screenshots.
- No complex background textures, shadows, gradients, or realistic items.
- Do not attempt to label every single node or detail.
- Do not place a category title (e.g., "Workflow / System Architecture / Pitfalls") in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic Direction

- Bizarre, creative, thought-provoking, clean, and imaginative.
- Not cute, childish, overly complex, or rigid.
